<p><p>The degradable film market comprises primarily two types: edible films and water-soluble films. Edible films are designed for food packaging, enhancing shelf life while being safe for consumption. They are often made from natural polymers, such as proteins or polysaccharides. Water-soluble films, on the other hand, dissolve in water and are used in applications like single-use packaging or in laundry detergents. Both types focus on sustainability, minimizing environmental impact and offering biodegradable alternatives to traditional plastics.</p></p>
