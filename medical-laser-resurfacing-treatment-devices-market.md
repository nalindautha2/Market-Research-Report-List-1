<p><p>Medical laser resurfacing treatment devices are categorized into two main types: ablative lasers and nonablative lasers. Ablative lasers remove the outer layers of skin, promoting significant rejuvenation and addressing deeper issues like scars and wrinkles. These devices typically require downtime for recovery. In contrast, nonablative lasers penetrate the skin without removing its surface, stimulating collagen production and improving skin texture with minimal downtime. Both types are effective for various skin conditions, offering tailored solutions for patients seeking skin improvement.</p></p>
